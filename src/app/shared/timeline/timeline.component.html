Our History

1886

Karl Benz creates the first gasoline-powered car, the Benz Patent-Motorwagen.

1901

The first car to be called a Mercedes is released by Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft (DMG).

1926

Mercedes-Benz is officially founded following the merger of Karl Benz's and Gottlieb Daimler's companies.

1936

Mercedes-Benz introduces the 260D, the world's first diesel-powered passenger car.

1954

The Mercedes-Benz 300 SL "Gullwing" is unveiled, featuring innovative gullwing doors and fuel injection.

1963

The iconic Mercedes-Benz 600, known as the "Grosser Mercedes," is launched as a luxury limousine.

1979

Introduction of the Mercedes-Benz G-Class, a durable and versatile off-road vehicle.

1993

The C-Class is introduced, replacing the 190 series as the entry-level Mercedes-Benz model.

1997

Mercedes-Benz unveils the A-Class, marking its entry into the compact car market.

2010

The SLS AMG is launched, featuring gullwing doors and becoming the first car designed entirely by AMG.

2014

Mercedes-Benz introduces the GLA, a compact crossover SUV.

2020

The EQC, the first fully electric vehicle from Mercedes-Benz's EQ brand, is launched.
